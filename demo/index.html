Design engineering playground

Details that compound.

A one-page demo of the principles from the emil-design-eng skill. Press, hover, and drag. Every transition here is intentional — custom easings, asymmetric timing, origin-aware transforms.

1. Buttons feel the press

transform: scale(0.97) on :active, custom ease-out, under 200ms. Buttons must feel responsive.

2. Popovers scale from their trigger

transform-origin: top left, not center. Enters from scale(0.95) + opacity: 0 — nothing appears from nothing.

3. Tabs with a moving indicator

A single indicator element animates between tab positions. ease-in-out for on-screen movement; 280ms feels crisp.

4. Hold to delete

clip-path reveals a fill on press — 1600ms linear while deciding, 220ms ease-out on release. Asymmetric timing: slow where the user decides, fast where the system responds.

5. What you don't see

No animation on keyboard focus. Hover gated to fine pointers. Reduced motion collapses transforms but keeps opacity. Toasts use transitions (interruptible) not keyframes (restart from zero). Every easing is a cubic-bezier, not a built-in.
